### Project Overview
This dashboard covers probation statistics in Illinois. Probation is the largest component of the Illinois penalty system. The Administrative Office of the Illinois Courts (AOIC) supervises between 90,000 and 120,000 people at any given time.

This population dwarfs the prison system, which currently incarcerates approximately 30,000 people and supervises roughly 23,000 individuals on Mandatory Supervised Release (MSR) at any one time.

Probation is a versatile sentencing option utilized in lieu of incarceration. While prison can upend an individual's life by severing social ties, probation enables people to maintain their community connections and employment. It also allows the courts to tailor sentencing; for example, a court can mandate specific rehabilitative programs, such as drug treatment or mental health counseling, as a condition of supervision.

The Data
The data published by the AOIC and used in this dashboard are referred to as Legacy Statistics.

Historically, Illinois probation data has been collected through a decentralized "patchwork" of manual reporting from 102 counties. From 2011 to 2026, each of the state's 25 judicial circuits has published individual Excel workbooks on the AOIC website. Each workbook contains anywhere from 3 to 10 distinct sheets of data.

Ultimately, this has resulted in a repository of over 900 Excel files containing vital information on the Illinois probation system. This project aims to synthesize that massive, fragmented corpus into a unified data pipeline. This pipeline funnels raw legacy data into a streamlined dashboard and dataset designed to increase transparency, identify statewide trends, and provide researchers with a longitudinal view of community supervision.

The Transition to Modern Infrastructure
The AOIC is currently phasing out the Legacy Statistics system in favor of a more dynamic, real-time data integration model. However, as of 2026, Legacy Excels are still being published and remain the primary source for historical and current probation trends. This dashboard serves as the bridge between these two eras of data management.
